Front page story

September 21st, 2014, by Editorial Board | No Comments

This project is new. We’re building from scratch and taking all the help and criticism we can get.

“We need to talk.” No one likes to hear those words. They mean “we have a problem” but in this case they’re also the solution. They were also the general sentiment of a conversation we had over very bad—though surprisingly cheap—Chinese food in Montreal last February. In discussing our impressions of our first year at Queen’s Law, we found ourselves returning time and again to a desire for conversation, a need to talk; about the law, about our school, about our country and the world. We both disappointment with a lack of real conversation outside of the classroom. Perhaps after hours of lectures and scores more of reading, it’s reasonable to not want to continue hashing over those things in our spare time. That’s fair. But consider the opportunity before us: we’re surrounded by exceptionally intelligent people from all different backgrounds and all corners of our country and beyond. What better chance will we ever have to grapple with issues, spar with one another, and ultimately come to a better understanding of the world and each other?

That’s what we hope Juris Diction can be. A place to express our views and engage in respectful dialogue and disagreement in a safe and supportive atmosphere. A place to celebrate the accomplishments and initiatives of our peers. A place for upper years to enlighten 1Ls with the wisdom gleaned by sleepless nights poring over readings and job applications. A place to escape the study of law and connect with the culture around us. A place to laugh at ourselves.

Our vision for Juris Diction is one that takes the commitment to quality and journalistic integrity of traditional media and combines that with the sense of community fostered by social media. We’ll be publishing entirely online and there will be multiple ways to engage with our content and writers: contributing response pieces, engaging on social media, and a series of in-person events aimed at taking the conversation off the screen and into true, human interaction. If you have a great idea of something you’d like to write or see written, we want to hear from you. Experience is required, but you have it. You’ve made it to law school. All you need to do is think and write.

This project is new. We’re building from scratch and taking all the help and criticism we can get, from anyone who wants to see this initiative succeed and better represent them. There will be unforeseen hurdles to cross in the coming months, potentially even some changes on the fly. What we can guarantee is that our content will make you think and we will strive to meet the highest standards week in and week out.

Before closing, some thanks to those who have helped us get this far. Firstly to our inaugural editorial and executive boards, thank you for jumping headfirst into this project before our vision was even complete and for your hard work and commitment over the summer. Thank you to Victor Temprano for putting together this beautiful website. [Include other people in here] Finally, a special thank you to my father, Ian Sadinsky, for the punny masterpiece that is the name Juris Diction.

We need to talk. We hope you join our conversation.
